Overview

The Medical Center is New Eden's primary healthcare facility, one of the five original buildings encircling the central roundabout. Though its exterior is identical to its neighbors—graceful curved facades of local stone composite—its interior is a fully equipped small hospital serving a colony population of approximately 600 souls.

Unlike the automated medical bays aboard starships, the Medical Center maintains a philosophy of human-centered care. While Utara handles diagnostics, monitoring, and routine data, the Center's small but dedicated staff ensures that healing remains a human interaction. This philosophy was tested during the Kret tribunals and affirmed under the leadership of Dr. Alex Cruiz.

Key Personnel

Dr. Alex Cruiz — Chief of Medicine

Dr. Cruiz arrived on New Eden from the orbital station Utopis with his wife Dee, shortly after the exile of Dr. Douglas Kret. His move was both personal and professional: his close friends Amerigo Carisco and his daughter Celectra had already settled on the surface, and the Medical Center needed a leader who could restore trust after Kret's tribunals.

A true jack-of-all-specialties, Cruiz performs surgery, delivers babies, sets bones, treats chronic conditions, and counsels the dying. In a colony of 600, there is no room for narrow specialization—he is physician, surgeon, pediatrician, and gerontologist by turns, often in the same day.

He delivered Celectra on October 11, 2225, aboard Utopis—catching her in his own hands before the automated systems could react. Seven years later, she would call him "Uncle Doc." By Book 2, it was simply "Uncle Alex." And in November of 2247, in this very building, he would deliver her twins.

— From "A History of New Eden Medicine"

Terry Fairchild — Head Nurse

Terry Fairchild (agender; uses they/them) is the backbone of the Medical Center. As head nurse, they manage day-to-day operations, train new staff, and maintain the delicate balance between Utara's monitoring and human touch.

But Terry's most defining moment came before Cruiz arrived. During Dr. Douglas Kret's tenure, Terry risked their entire career to:

  • Defend Celectra Carisco-Bowers when Kret's unethical practices endangered her
  • Testify truthfully against their own boss during the Book 1 tribunal

Their testimony was instrumental in Kret's removal. When Cruiz took over, he kept Terry as head nurse—not out of obligation, but because they were the most competent, most ethical person in the building. The two now run the Center as equals: Cruiz the hands, Fairchild the memory of why trust matters.

"I didn't risk my career to get rid of Kret so I could watch someone else make the same mistakes. Dr. Cruiz never would." — Terry Fairchild

Facilities & Capabilities

Though modest by Earth standards, the Medical Center is a fully functional small hospital. Its capabilities are optimized for a population of 600, with flexibility for surges (construction accidents, exploration injuries, or the occasional epidemic).

Department Capabilities
Emergency & Trauma 3 fully equipped autodoc suites; capable of handling industrial accidents, severe injuries, and critical care
Surgical Suite 2 operating rooms (one robotic-assisted, one manual); Cruiz performs everything from appendectomies to C-sections
Maternity Wing 4 birthing suites; home to the colony's newest citizens (including Celectra's twins in 2247)
Inpatient Ward 12 beds for overnight or extended stays; rarely full except during illness outbreaks
Outpatient Clinic Daily walk-in hours for routine care, chronic condition management, and checkups
Rehabilitation Center Physical therapy, hydrotherapy, and Utara-guided motion tracking for recovery
Mental Health Sanctuary Private counseling rooms, meditation spaces; the only place with guaranteed human-to-human therapy
Pharmacy & Synthesis On-site medical synthesis for medications too specialized for home units
Diagnostic Imaging Full suite (MRI, CT, ultrasound) integrated with Utara's analysis

Staffing limitations mean not all departments are active simultaneously—Cruiz and Fairchild prioritize based on need, shifting resources as the colony demands.

The Kret Era & Transition

Dr. Douglas Kret

Before Alex Cruiz, there was Douglas Kret—the original Chief of Medicine and holder of Council Seat 3. Kret's tenure ended in disgrace following two tribunals (Books 1 and 2) led by Security Chief Evelyn Roan. His crimes included medical ethics violations, unauthorized experimentation, and endangering patients—including, critically, Celectra Carisco-Bowers.

Terry Fairchild's testimony was pivotal in exposing Kret's practices. After his removal from the council and medical leadership, Kret was exiled to roam the moon with a survey team, serving as medic and cook—a steep fall from his former status. His departure left a void that Alex Cruiz would fill.

Cruiz's Arrival

Alex Cruiz moved down from Utopis with his wife Dee shortly after Amerigo and Celectra settled on New Eden. The timing was intentional: his friends needed him, and the colony needed a healer it could trust. Cruiz stepped into Seat 3 on the council and began the slow work of rebuilding the Medical Center's reputation.

Unlike Kret, who hoarded authority, Cruiz immediately elevated Terry Fairchild to Head Nurse and made clear that the Center operated on transparency and consent. His first act was to invite any patient of Kret's to request a full review of their treatment—no questions asked.

📊 ANI Comfort Monitoring (Late Book 3)

Late in Book 3, Dr. Cruiz began implementing a revolutionary application of ANI (Artificial Neural Interface) technology. Previously used for basic neural monitoring, Cruiz adapted the tech to allow Utara to track not just patients' vital signs, but their actual perceived comfort level.

This innovation means:

  • Real-time pain assessment: Utara detects discomfort before the patient can verbalize it
  • Personalized sedation: ANI adjusts anesthetic delivery based on individual neural responses
  • Recovery optimization: Comfort data helps Cruiz fine-tune post-operative care
  • End-of-life dignity: Terminal patients can communicate comfort needs even when verbal communication fails

Cruiz insisted on rigorous consent protocols—ANI monitoring is always optional, and patients can override or disconnect at any time. The technology represents the opposite of Kret's philosophy: monitoring in service of the patient, not in spite of them.

The two deliveries, separated by 22 years, bookend Cruiz's relationship with the Carisco-Bowers family. In 2225, he caught Celectra on Utopis—a moment he describes as "the first time I held my heart outside my body." By 2247, Celectra was a woman, an ambassador, and a mother, and Cruiz was there to welcome the next generation.

The twin delivery was notable for another reason: it was the first time Cruiz used ANI comfort monitoring on a conscious, laboring patient. Celectra's real-time feedback helped refine the technology, and both babies (a boy and a girl) arrived healthy, with their uncle—now great-uncle—beaming through tears.

Council Seat 3: Medicine

The Chief of Medicine holds one of the original 12 voting seats on New Eden's council. The transition of this seat represents one of the colony's most dramatic political shifts.

Period Incumbent Status
3 Pre-Book 1 Douglas Kret Removed by tribunal (ethics violations)
3 Book 1 onward Dr. Alex Cruiz Appointed after Kret's removal

As a council member, Cruiz advocates for:

  • Mental health funding and staff
  • Preventive medicine programs
  • Ethical oversight of medical research
  • Patient autonomy in all treatments

He is notably quiet during debates outside his expertise, but when medicine is discussed, his voice carries weight—both because of his competence and because no one wants to repeat the Kret era.

Life in a Small-Colony Hospital

The Jack-of-All-Specialties Reality

With a colony population of 600 or less (varying by book), the Medical Center cannot support specialists. Dr. Cruiz is typical of colony physicians across the stellar system: expected to deliver babies, set fractures, perform surgery, treat infections, counsel the depressed, and comfort the dying—sometimes all in the same shift.

This reality was true under Kret and remains true under Cruiz. What changed was attitude: Kret saw it as a burden; Cruiz sees it as the privilege of being present for every stage of human life.

A Typical Day

  • 0600: Utara handoff from overnight monitoring; review alerts
  • 0700-0900: Rounds on inpatients (rarely more than 3-4)
  • 0900-1200: Outpatient clinic (routine checkups, chronic care)
  • 1200-1300: Lunch with Terry, discussing cases and colony gossip
  • 1300-1700: Procedures, surgeries, or home visits (if needed)
  • 1700-1900: Paperwork, research, ANI data review
  • On call: 24/7 for emergencies—Terry shares this duty

Staffing Limitations

The Medical Center operates with a lean team:

  • 1 physician (Cruiz)
  • 1 head nurse (Fairchild)
  • 2-3 rotating nurses (cross-trained from other sectors)
  • Utara (constant monitoring, diagnostics, records)
  • Volunteer aides (community members training in basic care)

During crises (accidents, epidemics), the colony mobilizes—off-duty engineers assist with logistics, teachers help with childcare, and everyone understands that the Medical Center's small size requires community support.

"Uncle Doc" to "Uncle Alex"

Celectra Carisco-Bowers' relationship with Dr. Cruiz is one of the emotional anchors of the series. The evolution of her address marks their deepening bond:

  • Book 1: "Uncle Doc" — Formal, respectful, still processing his arrival after Kret's departure
  • Book 2 onward: "Uncle Alex" — Familiar, affectionate, the title of true family

After her mother's death (before the series) and with her father Amerigo often absorbed in science, Celectra found in Alex and Dee Cruiz a second family. The Medical Center became a place of safety—not just for her body, but for her spirit. Even as ambassador, she stops by weekly to sit in his office, drink tea, and let someone who knew her before she was extraordinary simply see her.

When I was seven, I asked Uncle Doc if he'd be my doctor forever. He said, 'I plan to be your doctor until you're old enough to be my doctor.' I didn't understand then. Now I do.

— Celectra Carisco-Bowers, Book 5

Cultural Significance

The Medical Center represents more than healthcare—it embodies New Eden's commitment to trust, transparency, and human connection. The transition from Kret to Cruiz was a moral reset for the colony, proving that even those in power can be held accountable.

In the Novels

  • Book 1: The Medical Center is the site of Kret's unethical practices and the gathering of evidence for his tribunal.
  • Throughout: Cruiz's quiet competence provides contrast to political drama elsewhere.
  • Late Book 3: ANI innovation showcases the colony's technological progress and ethical rigor.
  • Book 3 climax: The twin delivery scene—tender, joyful, and deeply human—reminds readers what the colony is fighting to preserve.

The Terry Fairchild Legacy

Terry Fairchild's courage in testifying against Kret set a precedent: in New Eden, speaking truth to power is not just permitted—it's honored. They remain the quiet conscience of the Medical Center, and any young colonist considering a medical career is sent to talk with Terry first.
