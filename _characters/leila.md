### Overview
Leila Al‑Farsi is a New Eden‑born martial artist and grappling specialist, slightly younger than Aulii’s generation but older than Celectra’s. She reached the semifinal of the Book 2 Sundome Karate Tournament, losing to Celectra Carisco in a close, tactical match. Known for her deceptive timing and mastery of throws, she later becomes the colony’s leading instructor of break‑falling and falling safety – skills that prove essential for resonance chamber work and field operations.
</div>
<div class="card-bg" markdown="1">
### Physical Description
Leila has a compact, strong build – low center of gravity, solid core, and powerful hips. Her dark hair is often tied back in a practical ponytail, and her brown eyes have a calm, assessing quality. She moves with deliberate economy, rarely wasting energy. When she throws, the motion looks almost effortless, the result of thousands of repetitions. She prefers simple, durable training clothes and wears a well‑worn black belt that she has earned over many years.
</div>
<div class="card-bg" markdown="1">
### Personality & Skills
Leila is defined by:
- deceptive timing and off‑balance setups
- a methodical, almost mathematical approach to grappling
- calm under pressure (never rushes a throw)
- quiet confidence in her technique
- a patient, tactile teaching style

Her strengths include:
- judo‑style throws and hip techniques
- ukemi (break‑falling) in unstable environments
- teaching safe falling to youth
- reading an opponent’s weight distribution

Her weaknesses include:
- weaker in stand‑up striking exchanges
- tendency to over‑prepare and under‑improvise
- sometimes too reserved for effective cheerleading
</div>
<div class="card-bg" markdown="1">
### Relationships
- **Celectra Carisco** – semifinal opponent who beat her with her own throw; Leila respects Celectra’s adaptability and they later collaborate on falling protocols
- **Aulii Vongphachanh** – colleague and co‑instructor; they share a precision‑focused philosophy
- **Jenni & Xara Carisco‑Bowers** – primary students in break‑fall training; both twins credit Leila with saving them from serious injury during the gallery rescue
- **Sensei Onishi Kisa** – mentor who encouraged her to develop her grappling specialty
- **Kai Vasquez & Amara Okonkwo** – cohort peers; she often spars with them to keep her timing sharp
</div>
<div class="card-bg" markdown="1">
### Story Overview (Non‑Spoiler)
Leila’s arc is about finding power in vulnerability – the ability to fall safely and to use an opponent’s momentum against them. She loses to Celectra in a tournament but takes that loss as impetus to refine her specialty rather than broaden it. Through her teaching, she transforms ukemi from a niche martial art into a core survival skill for resonance‑era youth. She represents the generation that learns to embrace instability, turning every fall into a setup for the next movement.
</div>
