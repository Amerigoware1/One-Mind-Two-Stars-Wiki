# {{ page.title }}

## Overview
Dee Cruiz is a long‑time member of the New Eden community and the wife of Dr. Alexander “Alex” Cruiz. Known for her warmth, humor, and steady presence, she becomes an unofficial aunt to the Carisco children and a beloved figure in the colony. Dee is the kind of person who makes any room feel safer simply by being in it.

## Physical Description
Dee has a soft, expressive face, warm brown skin, and a distinctive voice — low, airy, and instantly recognizable. She carries herself with relaxed confidence, often wearing practical clothing with small personal touches of color or pattern. Her smile is gentle but knowing, and her eyes miss nothing.

## Personality & Skills
Dee is defined by:

- kindness that never feels performative  
- a grounded, unhurried way of speaking  
- quiet emotional intelligence  
- a talent for making people feel seen  
- a sense of humor that sneaks up on you  

Her strengths include:

- community organizing  
- conflict de-escalation  
- supporting overworked medical staff  
- being the emotional “soft landing” for the Cruiz and Carisco families  

She is the person Alex decompresses with after long shifts, and the one Zoë trusts with the emotional undercurrents of the colony.

## Relationships
- **Dr. Alexander Cruiz** — husband, lifelong partner  
- **Zoë Carisco** — close friend  
- **Amerigo Carisco** — friend through Alex  
- **Celectra Carisco** — “niece,” someone she has loved since Celectra was seven  
- **Xan Carisco** — another honorary nephew  
- **Jenni Jo & Xara Zo** — grand-nieces she spoils shamelessly  
- **Ripple** — a Galinstanian who enjoys her calm presence  

## Story Overview (Non-Spoiler)
Dee’s role in the series is subtle but essential. She provides emotional grounding for Alex, stability for the Carisco family, and a sense of continuity as the colony grows and changes. She is not a scientist or a council member, but her influence is felt in the quiet spaces — the kitchen table conversations, the late-night check-ins, the moments when someone needs a steady hand.
