Overview
Dr. Lorain Troy served as the Chief Scientist of New Eden Colony for 35 years, providing steady leadership through the colony's most challenging periods of adaptation and first contact. A brilliant xenobiologist with deep ethical convictions, she was instrumental in establishing the scientific framework for human–Galinstanian relations. Her retirement in late October 2246 marked the end of an era and paved the way for Amerigo Carisco's succession.
Physical Description
Dr. Troy carries herself with the poised authority of someone accustomed to responsibility. She has silver-streaked dark hair kept in a practical but elegant style suitable for both council chambers and lab inspections. Her face bears the lines of both concern and warmth, with observant eyes that miss little in scientific proceedings. She typically wears functional but well-maintained colonial attire, often with subtle markers of office such as a council pin or data tablet.
Personality & Skills
Dr. Troy is defined by:
ethical rigor in scientific practice
collaborative leadership style
long-term sustainability focus over rapid expansion
diplomatic firmness when principles are challenged
mentorship of next-generation scientists
Her strengths include:
xenobiology and cross-species relations expertise
establishing non-invasive study methods for indigenous life
mediating between scientific and political interests
maintaining institutional stability through crises
Her weaknesses include:

Relationships

Story Overview (Non-Spoiler)
Dr. Troy's arc is about the weight of scientific leadership—holding a colony's research ethics steady when every discovery carries existential stakes. She represents the voice that asks "Should we?" not just "Can we?" Her retirement isn't an ending, but a passing of the torch to a generation she helped shape. Her legacy is measured not in discoveries, but in the ethical framework that guides all future research.

<div id="book1" class="tab-content">
<h2>Book 1 — Chief Scientist (Age 42)</h2>
In 2232, Dr. Troy serves as Chief Scientist during the aftermath of Dr. Kret's unethical experiments on Celectra Carisco. She provides expert testimony during Kret's tribunal, helping establish the precedent that scientific knowledge gained through violation must be acknowledged but never repeated. She develops the first xenobiology protocols for Ares, emphasizing non-invasive study methods for indigenous life. Her leadership during this crisis helps establish the colony's scientific ethics framework.
</div>

<div id="book2" class="tab-content">
<h2>Book 2 — First Contact Framework (Age 50)</h2>
By 2239, Dr. Troy oversees the expansion of human–Galinstanian scientific cooperation. She chairs the committee that reviews Celectra's role as bridge, ensuring that her unique abilities are studied ethically rather than exploited. When the northern cluster is rescued from Kret's torture, Dr. Troy advocates for their psychological recovery alongside physical safety. She begins mentoring Amerigo Carisco as a potential successor, recognizing his balanced approach to science and ethics.
</div>

<div id="book3" class="tab-content">
<h2>Book 3 — Retirement & Succession (Age 56)</h2>
In late October 2246, Dr. Troy announces her retirement after 35 years of service. A formal ceremony honors her contributions to xenobiology and cross-species relations. Amerigo Carisco is named her successor in a unanimous Council vote. She chairs the committee that reviews Celectra's Ph.D. defense ("Neuro-Interface Design for Cross-Species Communication"), ensuring rigorous standards while acknowledging the unprecedented nature of Celectra's work.

During the debate over using Dr. Kret's confiscated research, Dr. Troy offers a nuanced position: "Science doesn't have morality. Scientists do. We can learn from his methods while condemning his ethics. The knowledge exists. Pretending it doesn't doesn't undo the harm he caused—but we must never forget where it came from or what it cost."

Her retirement speech becomes a touchstone for colony science: "The greatest measure of our science will not be what we discover, but how we choose to use that knowledge. We have built bridges between species; may we continue to build them between our ethics and our ambitions."

</div>

<div id="book4" class="tab-content">
<h2>Book 4 — Elder Stateswoman (Age 65)</h2>
At 65, Dr. Troy serves as a Senior Council Member and advisor to the Science Division. She is consulted on major policy decisions but no longer handles day-to-day research administration. During the Four Rivers Resonance crisis, she supports the Resonance Stewardship Program's establishment, recognizing it as the institutional evolution she always hoped for.

She attends the Samaya Gallery opening (March 2256) as an honored guest, reflecting on how far colony science has come—from cautious first contact to genuine partnership. Her relationship with Amerigo remains warm; she offers guidance when asked but respects his autonomy as Chief Scientist.

</div>

<div id="book5" class="tab-content">
<h2>Book 5 — Living Legacy (Age 72)</h2>
At 72, Dr. Troy is fully retired but remains a respected elder in the colony. The ethical frameworks she established continue to guide New Eden's research—particularly the protocols for consent, transparency, and indigenous rights that proved crucial during the Resonant Echo rescue.

When the twins testify before Council about their discovery, Dr. Troy observes with quiet pride. She recognizes in their work the same values she championed: listening before acting, respecting autonomy, and building systems that protect the vulnerable.

Her arc culminates not in dramatic action, but in the satisfaction of knowing that the ethical foundation she built continues to serve the colony—long after she stepped down from leadership. As she told Celectra during her Ph.D. defense: "You are becoming what you always were meant to be—the architect, not the structure. The teacher, not the eternal student." The same words apply to her own legacy.
