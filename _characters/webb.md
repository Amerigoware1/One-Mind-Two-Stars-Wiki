# {{ page.title }}

## Overview
Dr. Nathaniel Webb is one of New Eden’s original agricultural scientists and a founding-generation colonist. Gruff, opinionated, and famously unimpressed by most things, Webb represents the early human mindset on Ares — pragmatic, territorial, and deeply skeptical of anything that threatened the colony’s fragile stability.

Over the course of the series, Webb evolves from a man with a colonialist outlook to someone who quietly, stubbornly grows. He becomes an unexpected emotional anchor during Celectra and Jaxon’s wedding, which he officiates with a mixture of dry humor and genuine affection.

## Physical Description
Webb is tall, weathered, and built like someone who has spent decades outdoors. His hair is white and perpetually wind‑tossed, and his eyebrows seem to have declared independence long ago. His voice is gravelly, his posture stiff, and his expression usually hovers somewhere between “mildly annoyed” and “deeply unimpressed.”

Despite this, his eyes soften around the Carisco family, especially the twins.

## Personality & Skills
Webb is defined by:

- a grumpy exterior that hides a steady moral core  
- blunt honesty  
- old‑world practicality  
- a slow but sincere capacity for change  
- deep loyalty to the colony’s survival  

His strengths include:

- agricultural systems engineering  
- soil chemistry  
- long-term settlement planning  
- crisis logistics  
- stubbornness (weaponized for good)  

His weaknesses include:

- outdated assumptions  
- difficulty admitting he’s wrong  
- a tendency to lecture when nervous  

## Relationships
- **Celectra Carisco** — someone he respects deeply, even if he doesn’t always understand her  
- **Jaxon Bowers** — a young man he likes more than he admits  
- **Zoë & Amerigo Carisco** — long-time colleagues  
- **Alex Cruiz** — frequent sparring partner in council debates  
- **Jenni Jo & Xara Zo** — children he pretends not to spoil  
- **Stan, Chime, Ripple** — Galinstanians he slowly learns to trust  

## Story Overview (Non-Spoiler)
Webb’s arc is about humility, growth, and the slow unlearning of assumptions. He begins as a man who sees Ares as a frontier to be tamed and ends as someone who understands that coexistence — not dominance — is the colony’s future.

His officiation of Celectra and Jaxon’s wedding becomes one of the most unexpectedly tender moments in the series.

  </div>

  <div id="book3" class="tab-content">
    <h2>Book 3 — The Wedding</h2>

  <div id="book4" class="tab-content">

He supports the Stewardship teams with agricultural logistics and helps maintain food stability during the disruptions. His relationship with the twins deepens as he becomes a kind of honorary grandfather.
