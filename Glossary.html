Martial Arts

Karate

Pronunciation: /k…ЩЋИr…СЋРti/

Kanji: з©ЇжЙЛ

Literally "empty hand," karate is a martial art practiced throughout the colonies. In the tradition of New Eden, karate is not about causing harm but about avoiding conflict altogetherвАФdeveloping the skill and control to neutralize threats with minimal damage when escape is necessary, and the wisdom to walk away when it is not. The true purpose of training is self-mastery: discipline, self-control, and the calm presence that prevents violence from arising. Children begin karate at age four to six, learning that the greatest victory is a fight that never happens. The only style taught is sundome (inch stop).

Shotokan

Pronunciation: /ЋИ Гo Кto КЋМk…СЋРn/

Kanji: жЭЊжґЫй§®

A traditional style of karate originating in Japan, founded by Gichin Funakoshi. Characterized by deep, linear stances and powerful techniques, Shotokan emphasizes fundamentals, kata, and kumite practiced with sundome control. It is the primary style taught in New Eden's dojos, valued for its emphasis on character development alongside physical skill.

Sundome

Pronunciation: /s КnЋИdo Кme…™/

Kanji: еѓЄж≠ҐгВБ

A traditional karate principle requiring practitioners to stop attacking techniques exactly 2.54 centimeters (one "sun") before contact. On Ares, it is the only form permitted. It requires "Kime" (focus)вАФthe ability to generate maximum kinetic energy and instantly arrest it through perfect muscular contraction. This discipline is taught from age four to ensure that power is always secondary to control.

Kata

Pronunciation: /ЋИk…СЋРt…Щ/

Kanji: еЮЛ

Detailed choreographed patterns of movements practiced solo. In New Eden, Kata is viewed as a "moving prayer" or meditation. It is the primary method for practicing the physics of Sundome, allowing students to master balance and transition in Ares's 0.95g environment without the variable of a partner.

Kumite

Pronunciation: /ЋИkuЋРm…™te…™/

Kanji: зµДжЙЛ

Sparring or partner practice in karate. Kumite ranges from structured, pre-arranged drills to free sparring, always practiced with sundome control to ensure safety. In New Eden's Activity Center, children begin with basic kumite drills before advancing to freer forms. The goal is not to defeat a partner but to learn timing, distance, and mutual respect.

Pronunciation: /ЋИse…™z…Щ/

Kanji: ж≠£еЇІ

A formal kneeling posture used in martial arts dojos for bowing, meditation, and moments of instruction. Practitioners kneel with legs folded beneath them, sitting on their heels with backs straight. On Ares, children learn seiza early as a foundation for discipline and respect in the dojo.

Hajime

Pronunciation: /h…СЋРЋИd Т…™me…™/

Kanji: еІЛгВБ

The command to begin. Used in martial arts to start a drill, sparring match, or kata. In the dojos of New Eden, children learn to respond instantly to "hajime" with focused readiness.

Obi

Pronunciation: /ЋИo Кbi/

Kanji: еЄѓ

The belt worn in martial arts to signify rank. Colors progress from white (beginner) through yellow, orange, green, blue, brown, and black. On Ares, children receive their first obi when they begin karate at age four. The belt represents not just technical skill but the growth of self-control, patience, and character.
