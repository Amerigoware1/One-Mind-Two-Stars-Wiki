Overview: The Hexagon

At the heart of New Eden, six buildings stand in a perfect ring around the colony's central roundabout. Five of these—the Medical Center, Science and Technology Center, Activity Center, Council Chamber, and The Nexus—are identical in their external architecture, constructed during the colony's founding as the original civic structures. A sixth, the Samaya Gallery and Museum of Integration, was added between Books 4 and 5, completing the hexagon and symbolizing the colony's cultural maturation.

While their exteriors echo one another in harmonious design, each building houses a distinct facet of colony life, together forming the complete ecosystem of New Eden society.

⚕️ Medical Center

"First, do no harm — then, heal completely." — Inscription above the main entrance

24/7 Operations Utara Diagnostic Integration Auto-Doc Suites Mental Wellness Wing

The New Eden Medical Center serves as the colony's primary healthcare facility, combining advanced autodoc technology with human-centered care. Unlike the purely automated medical bays aboard ships, the Center maintains a permanent staff of physicians, nurses, and mental health specialists who work alongside Utara's diagnostic capabilities.

Key Facilities

  • Emergency & Trauma Bay: Three fully equipped autodoc suites capable of handling anything from industrial accidents to critical care.
  • Wellness & Preventive Medicine: Regular health monitoring, genetic optimization tracking, and personalized fitness protocols.
  • Mental Health Sanctuary: Private counseling rooms, meditation spaces, and the only place in New Eden with guaranteed human-to-human therapeutic interaction.
  • Rehabilitation Center: Hydrotherapy, physical therapy with Utara-guided motion tracking, and recovery suites.
  • Pharmacy & Synthesis: On-site medical synthesis for medications too specialized for home units.

The Human Touch

While Utara handles diagnostics and routine monitoring, the Medical Center maintains that healing requires human presence. Terminal patients, those in psychological distress, and new parents are prioritized for human care. The Center's staff are among the most respected members of the colony.

🔬 Science and Technology Center

"Curiosity, codified." — Wall inscription

Research Labs Prototype Workshops Observation Dome Utara R&D Interface

The beating mind of New Eden, the Science and Technology Center is where the colony's brightest minds gather to push the boundaries of knowledge. Unlike the purely practical research conducted in agricultural or industrial settings, the Center focuses on pure science and long-term technological development.

Research Divisions

  • Astronomical Observatory: Located in the building's upper dome, tracking local star systems and deep-space phenomena.
  • Xenobiological Studies: Dedicated to understanding native flora and fauna (when applicable).
  • Materials Science: Developing new composites and industrial processes.
  • AI-Human Interaction Lab: Studying and improving the integration between Utara and colony life.
  • Junior Science Hall: After-school programs and youth research initiatives.

The Prototype Workshop

A fully equipped fabrication facility where researchers can build, test, and refine physical prototypes with direct Utara assistance. It's common to see engineers, biologists, and artists collaborating here on cross-disciplinary projects.

🏋️ Activity Center

"Strong bodies, stronger community." — Main hall motto

Olympic Pool Climbing Wall Courts & Fields Recovery Suites

The Activity Center serves as New Eden's comprehensive fitness and recreation facility. In a colony where physical labor is often optimized by machines, the Center ensures citizens maintain their health through deliberate exercise and, just as importantly, through play.

Athletic Facilities

  • Aquatics Wing: 50-meter Olympic pool, therapy pools, and water polo area.
  • Climbing & Bouldering: A 15-meter climbing wall with variable difficulty routes and auto-belays.
  • Multi-Sport Courts: Convertible spaces for basketball, volleyball, badminton, and futsal.
  • Cardio & Strength: Biometric-integrated equipment with Utara-guided form correction.
  • Yoga & Meditation Studio: Heated floors, soft lighting, and panoramic views of the roundabout.

Youth & Community Programs

The Center hosts after-school sports, adult leagues, and the annual New Eden Games — a week-long competition that brings the entire colony together. Its social spaces, including a smoothie bar and viewing lounge, make it a popular gathering spot even for non-athletes.

⚖️ Council Chamber

"Not law, but wisdom. Not rule, but consensus." — Chamber inscription

Debate Hall Public Gallery Utara Mediation Records Archive

The Council Chamber is New Eden's seat of governance — though "governance" here means facilitation rather than rule. The elected council meets in this circular hall to discuss colony-wide decisions, mediate disputes, and allocate resources. Unlike the Collaborative "Guild" Rooms in the Nexus, this space is reserved for formal deliberation.

Architecture

The Chamber is designed as a perfect circle, with seating in concentric rings — no head of the table, no position of authority. Council members sit among the citizens who attend sessions, and Utara's holographic presence manifests at the exact center when mediation or data presentation is required.

Functions

  • Weekly Open Sessions: Any citizen may attend and, with sufficient support, address the council.
  • Resource Allocation: Debates on colony priorities and project funding.
  • Dispute Resolution: Mediation between citizens, guilds, or families.
  • Ceremonial Events: Holiday proclamations, recognition of achievements, and memorials.
  • Historical Archive: Complete records of every council session, searchable via Utara.

Notable Tradition

Before any vote, the presiding council member asks: "Utara, show us the long path." The AI then projects the potential outcomes of each choice across 10, 50, and 100 years — not to dictate the decision, but to ensure it's made with full awareness.

🖼️ Samaya Gallery and Museum of Integration

"Art is what makes us more than engineered." — Maya's dedication

Built Books 4-5 Sixth Building Cultural Archive Rotating Exhibits
Timeline: Constructed between Books 4 and 5, completing the hexagon of central buildings.

The Samaya Gallery represents New Eden's cultural coming-of-age. Named after the colony's first dedicated artist (see Samaya), it serves as both a museum of human creativity and a living space for new artistic expression. Its addition to the central roundabout transformed the building cluster from purely functional to fully human.

Permanent Collections

  • Earth Legacy Wing: Digital recreations of humanity's greatest artworks, curated with Solid-Light projection for authentic viewing.
  • Integration Hall: The story of humanity and Utara's co-evolution, told through interactive exhibits.
  • Samaya Wing: Original works by the colony's first artist, preserved in their physical form.
  • Children's Gallery: Rotating exhibitions of youth artwork from New Eden's schools.

Active Spaces

  • Artist-in-Residence Studio: Working artists create (and invite public observation) in a glass-walled space.
  • Performance Hall: Intimate concerts, poetry readings, and theatrical performances.
  • Community Canvas: A large-format digital wall where anyone may contribute to an evolving collective artwork.
  • Utara's Art Corner: The AI's own algorithmic explorations of creativity, updated daily.

Cultural Significance

The Gallery's construction marked a turning point in New Eden's psychology — from survival and efficiency toward expression and reflection. It is the youngest of the six buildings but already among the most beloved.

The Central Roundabout

The six buildings are arranged around a beautifully landscaped circular plaza, the de facto address for any visitor: "Meet me at the roundabout."

Features

  • Central Fountain: A kinetic water sculpture that doubles as a cooling mist installation in summer.
  • Pathways: Radial spokes connect each building, with benches and native plantings.
  • Event Space: The roundabout hosts outdoor markets, festivals, and the annual New Eden Day celebration.
  • Utara Kiosks: Information points where the AI can provide directions, event schedules, or simply chat.
  • Time Capsule: A sealed vault beneath the fountain, containing messages from the colony's founders to be opened in 100 years.

The View

From any building's entrance, one sees the others in harmonious arrangement — a constant visual reminder that health, knowledge, activity, governance, community, and art form a complete circle. No building is more important than another; all are necessary.

Collective Significance

Together, these six buildings represent the full spectrum of New Eden society:

  • Medical Center — Care for the body
  • Science Center — Nourishment of the mind
  • Activity Center — Strength through play
  • Council Chamber — Wisdom through discourse
  • The Nexus — Joy through community
  • Samaya Gallery — Expression through art

No single building can stand alone — like the colony itself, they function only in concert. The roundabout that connects them is not just a traffic circle but the physical manifestation of New Eden's philosophy: that a complete human being, and a complete society, requires all six.

In the Novels

  • Books 1-4: The original five buildings appear throughout as settings for key scenes — council debates, medical emergencies, scientific breakthroughs, social gatherings, and athletic competitions.
  • Between Books 4 & 5: The Samaya Gallery is constructed, funded by the colony's first art endowment and designed by the architect who trained under the original builders.
  • Book 5 onward: The complete hexagon features prominently, with characters often crossing the roundabout from one building to another, symbolizing the integration of art into previously utilitarian colony life.
